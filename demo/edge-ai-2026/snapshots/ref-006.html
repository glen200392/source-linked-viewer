Adaptive Mixed-Precision Quantization for Transformer Architectures

Wei Chen, Yuki Tanaka, Priya Sharma, James Liu
Google Research • February 2026 • arXiv:2026.05678

Abstract

We present a novel adaptive mixed-precision quantization framework that automatically determines optimal bit-width allocation for each layer in transformer architectures. We demonstrate that adaptive mixed-precision quantization achieves near-lossless compression ratios of 8:1 across a wide range of transformer architectures, including BERT, GPT, and Vision Transformers. Our method outperforms uniform quantization by 12-18% in accuracy preservation while maintaining comparable inference speed.

1. Introduction

The deployment of large transformer models on edge devices remains challenging due to their substantial memory and computational requirements. Current quantization approaches typically apply uniform bit-width across all layers, ignoring the varying sensitivity of different architectural components.

In this work, we propose AdaptQ, a framework that leverages second-order sensitivity analysis to determine layer-wise optimal quantization precision, achieving compression ratios of 8:1 with less than 2% accuracy degradation.

2. Method

Our approach consists of three stages:

  1. Sensitivity Analysis: Compute Hessian-based sensitivity scores for each layer
  2. Bit-Width Allocation: Solve constrained optimization for optimal precision distribution
  3. Fine-Tuning: Knowledge distillation with quantization-aware training
